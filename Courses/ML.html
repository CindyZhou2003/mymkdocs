    Binomial distribution äºŒé¡¹åˆ†å¸ƒ¶

    1. Trials: The distribution is defined by the number of trials, n, which is a fixed number.

    2. Success Probability: The probability of success on an individual trial, p.

    3. Random Variable: A random variable X* following a Binomial distribution represents the number of successes in n* trials.

    4. Mean and Variance:

    5. Mean (Expected Value): E(X)=n*p

    6. Variance: Var(X)=np(1âˆ’p)

    7. Probability Mass Function (PMF): The probability of getting exactly k successes in n trials is given by: $$ \begin{align} P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \end{align} $$

    8. Usage: The Binomial distribution is used widely in statistics, particularly for modeling the number of successes in various scenarios such as flipping a coin, quality control processes, survey responses, and other fields where the outcomes are binary and trials are independent.
